# Topic Analysis
*The US ought to vest ecosystems with legal rights.*
## Rights of Nature
What's the difference between the use of "moral" vs "legal" in the resolution?
* Moral rights are not necessarily enforceable
* Legal rights have to be consistently applied
* "rights to exist" vary
### River example
* Ecosystem had been suffering for a while
* CAFOs created a lot of waste, taken to soybean and corn fields
* Flows into Erie, increases phosphorus content and facilitates algae growth
* Lake Erie Bill of Rights -- rights to exist, flourish, and naturally evolve
* Corporations send representatives and can invoke lawsuits; Erie similar
* Constitutional puzzle - corporations have a right to do business, lake has a right to be healthy. Who wins?
#### Main Arguments For
* Nature should have the right to exist, flourish, and naturally evolve
* Can help for environmental progress in places where existing laws can't reach
#### Main Arguments Against
* Who advocates for nature? 
* Can go against existing rights; who wins?
